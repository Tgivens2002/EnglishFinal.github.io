Racism in the American High School Education System

By Tyson Givens

Young children have extremely fragile minds that are capable of being manipulated to mirror their surrounding environments. Racism present within the United States’ high school educational system has had a cascading effect on the growth and development of our nation's youth. This systematic cultural oppression inevitably leads to harmful behaviors and beliefs surrounding racism and how it affects people. It is essential that we create and instill an inclusive education system that promotes positivity and equality.

Personal Stories

Aaliyah El-Amin

Aaliyah El-Amin remembers the afternoon she was tutoring a fourth-grade student on math problems. The student stopped, touched her arm, and asked if she ever wished she wasn’t black. The question was a painful one, which El-Amin recognized as the young girl trying to reconcile negative messages she heard about her race in our society.

Lakeview High School Teacher

Research findings from Lakeview High School indicate that most white teachers operated from an impaired consciousness about racism; that a majority "blamed the victim," assigning causality for racism to Blacks. Findings further indicate that of those who witnessed racist behavior by their white colleagues, the majority remained silent and did not challenge such behavior.

Final Words

Education and its effects on the young minds of the children that are trying to find their place in this world is not something that can be easily overlooked. The issues caused by segregation have trickled down to modern society, and until we learn from our history, we are bound to repeat it. Instead of leading students to higher chances of stereotyping and racist beliefs through a biased curriculum and oblivious educators, The United States needs to re-evaluate how and what its students are taught regarding racial tolerance. Students of color must be given the same opportunities as their white peers and as equality prevails our society as a whole will become one of inclusion.
